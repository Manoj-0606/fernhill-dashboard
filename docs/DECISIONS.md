# Fernhill Hotels Dashboard – Data Cleaning Decisions & Engineering Rationale

## Project Overview

The objective of this project was to transform the provided Fernhill Hotels booking dataset into a reliable dataset that could be used to answer three business questions:

1. How is each property performing?
2. Which booking channels are generating the most business?
3. What is the overall health score of each property?

Before building the dashboard, I performed exploratory data analysis on the raw dataset. During this process, I identified multiple data quality issues that would have affected revenue calculations, booking statistics and business insights if left unresolved.

The dashboard uses only the cleaned dataset generated after applying the decisions described below.

---

# Data Quality Issues Found

## 1. Missing Booking Channels (29 records)

### Issue

Twenty-nine bookings did not contain a booking channel.

Since the dashboard compares revenue generated by different booking channels, leaving these values blank would either remove bookings from the analysis or create misleading charts.

### Decision

Instead of deleting these bookings, I replaced missing values with **"Unknown"**.

### Reason

The booking itself is still valid. The only missing information is the acquisition source. Preserving these rows avoids unnecessary data loss while making incomplete records clearly visible.

---

## 2. Missing Nightly Rate (9 records)

### Issue

Nine bookings were missing the nightly room rate.

Without this value, revenue validation becomes impossible.

### Decision

Where possible, missing nightly rates were derived from:

Total Amount ÷ Number of Nights

If this could not be validated, the record was excluded from revenue calculations.

### Reason

Revenue is one of the primary KPIs shown on the dashboard, therefore incorrect financial values were not allowed to influence business insights.

---

## 3. Missing Total Amount (3 records)

### Issue

Three bookings did not contain the total booking amount.

### Decision

The missing value was recalculated using:

Nightly Rate × Number of Nights

### Reason

Both required values already existed for these bookings, making the revenue calculation deterministic.

---

## 4. Duplicate Booking IDs (8 records)

### Issue

Eight booking IDs appeared more than once.

Booking IDs should uniquely identify individual reservations.

### Decision

Duplicate booking IDs were investigated and duplicate records were removed while preserving the valid booking.

### Reason

Keeping duplicates would artificially increase:

- Revenue
- Booking count
- Property performance
- Health Score

---

## 5. Duplicate Rows (8 records)

### Issue

The dataset also contained completely duplicated rows.

### Decision

Exact duplicate records were removed.

### Reason

These records contained no new information and would distort every business metric.

---

## 6. Inconsistent Property Names

### Issue

Property names were not represented consistently throughout the dataset.

Examples included differences in spelling, capitalization and formatting.

### Decision

Property names were standardized into a consistent naming convention before aggregation.

### Reason

Without standardization, the same hotel could appear as multiple properties in dashboard visualizations.

---

## 7. Inconsistent Booking Channel Names

### Issue

Booking channels contained inconsistent text formatting.

Examples included differences in capitalization and naming conventions.

### Decision

Booking channel values were standardized before aggregation.

Final channel categories include:

- Direct
- Corporate
- OTA-MMT
- OTA-Booking
- Walk-in
- Unknown

### Reason

Consistent naming ensures that revenue from the same booking source is grouped together.

---

## 8. Inconsistent Booking Status Values

### Issue

Booking status values contained inconsistent formatting.

Examples included different capitalization and inconsistent text labels.

### Decision

Status values were standardized into:

- Confirmed
- Checked-out
- Cancelled
- No-show

### Reason

Cancellation percentage and booking statistics depend on consistent status values.

---

## 9. Revenue Validation Errors (16 records)

### Issue

Several bookings contained incorrect revenue values.

The Total Amount did not equal:

Nightly Rate × Number of Nights

### Decision

Revenue values were recalculated using:

Total Amount = Nightly Rate × Nights

### Reason

Revenue is the most important business metric in the dashboard and therefore had to be internally consistent.

---

## 10. Negative Revenue Values (5 records)

### Issue

Five records contained negative revenue.

Negative hotel revenue is not meaningful in this business context.

### Decision

These records were treated as invalid and corrected where possible. Records that could not be validated were excluded.

### Reason

Negative values would incorrectly reduce property revenue and health scores.

---

## 11. Zero-Night Bookings (4 records)

### Issue

Some bookings had zero nights.

A hotel stay cannot have a duration of zero nights while still generating accommodation revenue.

### Decision

These records were reviewed individually and corrected where valid information existed. Invalid bookings were excluded.

### Reason

Average Stay is one of the dashboard KPIs and would be distorted by zero-night stays.

---

## 12. Mixed Date Formats

### Issue

Check-in dates appeared in multiple formats.

Examples included different separators and date ordering.

### Decision

All dates were converted into a single datetime format before analysis.

### Reason

Consistent dates are required for filtering, sorting and future trend analysis.

# Health Score Definition

The raw dataset did not contain any single metric representing overall property performance.

To provide management with an easy-to-understand indicator, I created a custom Health Score.

The Health Score combines four business factors.

- Revenue generated
- Number of bookings
- Average stay duration
- Cancellation rate

Each metric is first normalized onto a common scale.

Positive contributors:

- Higher revenue
- Higher booking count
- Longer average stay

Negative contributor:

- Higher cancellation rate

The normalized values are combined into a score ranging approximately between 0 and 100.

This allows management to compare overall property performance using a single metric while still retaining access to detailed KPIs.

# Dashboard Design Decisions

Instead of displaying every available column, I focused only on information required to answer the client's questions.

The final dashboard contains:

- Total Revenue
- Total Bookings
- Average Stay
- Cancellation Percentage
- Overall Health Score

Visualisations include:

- Revenue by Property
- Revenue by Booking Channel
- Property Health Score Table
- Property Performance Summary
- Business Insights

Business Insights automatically highlight:

- Best performing property
- Property needing attention
- Highest revenue booking channel
- Overall cancellation percentage

This reduces the time required for management to interpret the dashboard.

---

# Assumptions Made

During development, several assumptions were necessary.

- Every booking represents one completed reservation.
- Unknown booking channels are retained instead of deleted.
- Revenue recalculation uses Nightly Rate × Nights.
- Duplicate Booking IDs represent data quality issues rather than legitimate repeated stays.
- Health Score is intended as a management indicator rather than a financial accounting metric.

---

# Error Handling

The dashboard includes basic validation to improve reliability.

- Dynamic filtering across all visualisations.
- Graceful handling when filters return no matching records.
- Revenue displayed using Indian currency formatting.
- Tables update automatically after every filter selection.

# Conclusion

The final dashboard is built entirely from the cleaned dataset and directly answers the client's three business questions.

By identifying and resolving multiple data quality issues before analysis, the resulting KPIs and visualisations are significantly more reliable than those generated from the raw dataset.

The emphasis throughout this project was accuracy, transparency and business usefulness rather than simply producing attractive visualisations.
